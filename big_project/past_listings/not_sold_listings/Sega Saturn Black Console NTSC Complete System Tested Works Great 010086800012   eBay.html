    Product Description
    Get back to classic video gaming with the Sega Saturn console. Dual processors and a full-color display bring a classic gaming console to life, making for quick gameplay and visuals that can be enjoyed by up to two players thanks to the dual controller ports. The Sega Saturn console was the first to accept both CDs and cartridges, giving gamers a wide selection of play possibilities. There are more than 245 games available on the classic Sega gaming console, which has its own internal memory storage and specialized memory cartridges for additional storage. Named by IGN as one of the 25 best gaming consoles of all time, the Sega Saturn console is shaped in a simple rectangle that fits onto any shelf. Place games into the Sega gaming console by lifting the top lid and inserting them directly inside. The Sega Saturn helped shape video gaming, and remains one of the best systems ever produced. A classic gaming console never goes out of style.

    "Sega's Forgotten Console, Worth Another Look" by:
    Member id ghidorahtheking ( Feedback Score Of 9 )
    The Sega Saturn had an unfortunate time in the USA. It just never had the kind of popularity that the Sony Playstation did. This has led to the Saturn being mostly forgotten now. But make no mistake, it is a great system. There are many great games that were released on the Saturn. It's ability to handle 2D sprites and graphics was actually much stronger than the Playstation's, and this shows in the beautiful 2D visuals the Saturn can produce. Some of the fighting games available for it are almost arcade perfect, such as Marvel Super Heroes. Sadly, many of the Saturn's best games were only rel...Read full revieweased in Japan. The good news is that many of those games are available and not too expensive on the internet. If you're going to get a Saturn, get an Action Replay Plus to go with it so you can play Japanese games. Don't worry about not being able to read Japanese; many of the best games don't have much text. They are fighting games like Marvel Super Heroes vs Street Fighter, Pocket Fighter, and Vampire Savior. There are also a great many Japanese space-shooting games available. Of course, there are plenty of USA titles you can buy for the Saturn, such as Virtua Fighter, Sonic Jam, and Resident Evil. The Saturn also has an amazing variety of controllers and accessories for having been around only a short time. I recommend picking up an arcade stick for it, as they are pretty cheap and well-built.

    "If you do not buy a Saturn, Segata Sanshiro will kill you with his stare." by:
    Member id issad313 ( Feedback Score Of 88)
    The Sega Saturn is a great console that has sadly gotten an unfair legacy due to its commercial failure in North America and Western Europe. (It was a moderate success in Japan).

    But thankfully, the Saturn is being revived, and in retrospect, I think most would agree that the Saturn's game library is stronger than that of its more successful rivals, the PS1 and the N64. Although the Saturn was capable of 3D polygonal graphics every bit as good as the PS1 or N64 (see Virtua Fighter 2 or Sega Rally for an example of great graphics for the era). But most of its games were 2D. At the t...Read full reviewime, it was bad business. But looking back, and seeing how visually unappealing the games of the time were, (We didn't notice because we were too enthralled by the novelty of it all) we can see that the decision to make mostly 2D games was a very good one.

    If you like fighters, the Saturn is for you.

    If you like 2D shooters, the Saturn is for you.

    If you like RPGs, and you happen to know Japanese, the Saturn is for you. (Sega of America stupidly didn't release too many of them)

    The Saturn's visuals are amazing. Some of the fighters on the Saturn look even better than their Dreamcast ports.

    The PS1 became a success by riding the waves of the 3D gaming revolution. But the Saturn's game library is far more timeless, because it did not give as much heed to those cultural trends.

    Don't get me wrong. The PS1 is great. The N64 is great. But the Saturn is special and depending on your taste in games, it may very well be the best of the three.
